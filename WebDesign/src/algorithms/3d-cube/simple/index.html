3D Cube Projection

Algorithm Overview

This algorithm displays a six-faced cube (d6) with distinct numbers on each face and allows the user to change the viewpoint by rotating the cube around three coordinate axes (X, Y, and Z). The cube is represented by its 3D vertices and faces in model space, centered at the origin.

The algorithm works by applying a series of transformations to the cube’s vertices:

  1. Model Transform: Scale, rotate (around X, Y, Z axes), and translate the cube in 3D space to position and orient it as desired.
  2. View Transform: Apply a camera transformation that simulates the user’s point of view, positioning and orienting the camera relative to the cube.
  3. Projection: Project the 3D transformed vertices onto a 2D plane using a perspective projection matrix, which simulates depth and foreshortening by scaling points inversely proportional to their distance from the camera.
  4. Rasterization: Connect the projected 2D points to render the cube’s edges and faces on the screen, displaying the cube with correct perspective and orientation.

How It Works

  • Step 1: The user views a 3D cube with distinct numbers on each of its six faces.
  • Step 2: The user changes the viewpoint by rotating the cube around the X, Y, and Z coordinate axes using controls or input.
  • Step 3: The algorithm applies 3D transformations (model, view, and projection) to the cube’s vertices based on the user’s input.
  • Step 4: The transformed 3D points are projected onto a 2D plane using perspective projection to simulate depth.
  • Step 5: The cube’s edges and faces are rendered on the screen, updating dynamically as the user changes the viewpoint.
